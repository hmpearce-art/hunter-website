Shifting My Mindset

For most of my life, I sought validation from others. I cared about what people thought of me and how they saw me.

Then I came across a quote that changed my perspective:

“Why do you care if everyone likes you? You don’t even like everyone.”

That really stuck with me. I realized that seeking validation from others isn’t the path to true happiness. It’s about understanding and accepting yourself for who you are.

Building a Routine of Self-Discipline

With weight loss and volleyball, building a routine helped me create a sense of discipline and control in my life. I started looking forward to the gym and volleyball practices, which made it easier to get through tough times.

Knowing that something good was coming — a training session, a game, or just movement — made the rest of life feel more manageable.

Growth Beyond the Gym

This journey has been about more than just physical transformation. It’s been about mental growth too. Developing the self-discipline to stick with my goals has made me more confident, happier, and more content with who I am.

This is a journey that continues to shape who I am, and I’m excited for where it leads next.

Finding Peace in Nature

Nature has been a huge part of resetting my mind and stepping back from stress. These moments remind me to slow down, breathe, and appreciate how far I’ve come.
