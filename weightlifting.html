Starting with Small Steps

When I began my journey, I didn’t jump into extreme workouts. Instead, I started with simple habits like walking 10,000 steps a day and focusing on a caloric deficit. These small actions built the foundation for everything that came later.

I learned quickly that you don’t need complicated routines to lose weight. Consistency, discipline, and healthier decisions are what make the biggest impact.

“Gains are made in the gym, but weight loss happens in the kitchen.”

Weight Progress Milestones

This table shows the key stages in my progress. Even when the scale went up, the long-term trend was forward.

Stage Weight (lbs) Notes
Starting Point 260+ Lots of fluctuation while building new habits.
Mid Journey 240–245 Ups and downs, but still improving overall.
Current Range 218–226 Steady progress from discipline and nutrition.

Adapting to a Healthier Lifestyle

I made small but meaningful swaps in my daily life. I replaced sugary snacks with Tic Tacs, switched soda for Diet Coke, and had tea at night instead of a heavy meal. These small changes added up fast.

Hitting Milestones

Hitting 300 pounds was one of my biggest emotional victories. The gym slowly became something I looked forward to, not something I dreaded.
